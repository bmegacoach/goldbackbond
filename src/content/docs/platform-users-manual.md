## 1. What USDGB Is (and Is Not)

USDGB is a gold‑backed utility token pegged to **$1.00 worth of gold at spot price**, with reserves attested via a Monetization Memorandum and held through the USDGB Trust Fund.[file:2][file:4][file:7][file:8][file:11] You use USDGB by trading it on exchanges, staking it for on‑chain certificates, and borrowing against those certificates with participating institutional lenders.[file:7][file:8][file:11]  

USDGB is **not**:

- Equity in Goldbackbond Inc., Goldbackbond LTD, or the USDGB Trust Fund.[file:7][file:8][file:11]  
- A security token or claim on debentures or specific Trust assets.[file:7][file:8][file:11]  
- A claim to physical gold redemption; there is no mechanism to exchange tokens for bullion.[file:7][file:8][file:11]

## 7. Staking USDGB for Certificates

Staking converts your free USDGB into a **locked certificate** that can earn yield and serve as collateral for loans.[file:7][file:11]

### 7.1 How to Stake

1. **Connect your wallet** to the official Goldbackbond dApp.  
2. Navigate to the **Certificate Staking** section.  
3. Choose the amount of USDGB you want to lock.  
4. Review the current base APY and any applicable bonus pool terms.[file:7]  
5. Confirm the transaction in your wallet; the staking contract will lock your tokens and mint a **staking certificate** representing:  
   - Your address.  
   - The staked amount.  
   - The start time.  
   - The current unlock time.[file:7][file:11]  

Initially, certificates are created with a **365‑day lock term** from the staking date.[file:7][file:11]

### 7.2 Staking Terms: 1, 3, 5, or 10 Years

The staking contract supports **flexible long‑term commitments**:[file:7][file:11]

- **Minimum term:** 365 days (1 year) for all new stakes.  
- **Optional term extensions:** at any time after the initial stake, and especially when arranging a loan, you may extend the certificate’s term to **3, 5, or 10 years**.  
- **Resetting the unlock clock:**  
  - When you extend the term, the unlock time is reset based on the extension date, not the original stake date.  
  - Example: you stake on Jan 1, 2026 (1‑year term to Jan 1, 2027), then extend to 5 years on Dec 1, 2026; the new unlock date becomes Dec 1, 2031.  
- **One‑way direction:** you cannot shorten a certificate’s remaining term; extensions only move from shorter to longer lock durations.  

Participating lenders may **require** a longer term (3, 5, or 10 years) as a condition for offering certain loan sizes or durations.[file:7][file:8][file:11]

### 7.3 What You Earn from Staking

Base staking rewards are defined in the program parameters:

- A fixed target APY (for example, 9%) is configured in the staking contract.[file:7]  
- Rewards accrue continuously based on your staked amount and time.[file:7]  
- The contract calculates rewards daily and **pays them weekly** in USDGB.[file:7]  

Some pools may also offer a **gold‑price‑linked bonus APY** of up to 15%, based on the performance of gold relative to a baseline over rolling 30‑day windows.[file:7] Bonus terms are presented in the dApp and may require you to participate in specific liquidity or promotion programs.[file:7]

Rewards are funded from treasury operations and program reserves, not directly from new investor deposits.[file:7][file:11]

## 8. Borrowing Against Staking Certificates

Goldbackbond works with institutional lenders that accept staking certificates as collateral for loans.[file:7][file:11]

### 8.1 Basic Flow

1. **Stake USDGB** – you first create a staking certificate for your chosen amount.  
2. **Apply with a lender** – Goldbackbond or your sales representative introduces you to a participating lender, who conducts any required KYC and underwriting.[file:7][file:11]  
3. **On‑chain collateral check** – the lender verifies your certificate on‑chain (amount, term, and status).  
4. **Loan approval** – if approved, you receive a term sheet specifying:  
   - Loan amount (up to **70% LTV** of the USD value of your staked USDGB).  
   - Interest rate (often 8–12% annually).  
   - Duration (for example, 12–36 months) and payment schedule.[file:7]  
5. **Lien and disbursement** – your certificate is flagged in the staking contract as **encumbered by a lender lien**, and the lender wires funds to your bank account or sends stablecoins, depending on their policy.[file:7][file:11]  

Throughout the loan, your USDGB remains locked in the staking contract; you retain economic exposure to USDGB and any staking rewards per program terms, but you cannot unstake or transfer the locked principal while liens exist.[file:7][file:11]

### 8.2 Loan‑Driven Term Extensions

When you use a certificate as collateral:

- The lender may **require** that you extend the staking term to better align with the loan maturity (for example, extending a 1‑year certificate to 5 years).[file:7][file:8][file:11]  
- This extension is executed via the dApp and updates the certificate’s unlock time to extension date + chosen term.[file:7][file:11]  
- Once extended, you cannot later reduce the term, even if the loan is repaid early; the certificate remains locked until the new unlock date.[file:7][file:11]

This structure gives lenders predictable, long‑dated collateral while giving you leverage against your gold‑pegged holdings.[file:7][file:11]

### 8.3 What Happens If You Miss a Payment

The system uses a **contractual 30‑day grace period**, not price‑triggered liquidations.[file:7][file:8][file:11]

1. **Missed payment recorded** – when you miss a scheduled loan payment, the lender or its servicing agent notifies the guardian and marking of a **first missed‑payment timestamp** occurs on‑chain.[file:7][file:8]  
2. **30‑day late period starts** – from that date, a 30‑day countdown begins.  
3. **Daily risk notifications** – during the 30 days, the platform sends you daily alerts so you understand the risk of losing your collateral.[file:7][file:8]  
4. **Cure options** – during the late period, you may:  
   - Pay the missed amount and late fees.  
   - Refinance or restructure the loan with your lender.  
   - Reduce your exposure by paying down principal where allowed.[file:7][file:8][file:11]  
5. **Potential reassignment after 30 days** – if the default is **still uncured after 30 days**, or if other material default conditions defined in the loan agreement occur, the GUARDIAN_ROLE multisig may call the contract function that **reassigns your staking certificate to the lender**.[file:7][file:8]  

If reassigned:

- The lender becomes the new owner of the certificate and the right to withdraw the underlying USDGB when the lock term ends.[file:7][file:8]  
- Tokens are reassigned to the lender as collateral recovery; they are not automatically sold on exchanges by the protocol.[file:7][file:8]  

There are no automatic liquidation bots based on price; risk is tied to your payment behavior and loan terms, not market volatility alone.[file:7][web:38][web:46]

### 8.4 Loan Payoff and Lien Release

When you fully repay your loan (principal plus interest) and the lender confirms payoff:

- The lender or its servicing agent submits a **lien‑release instruction** to the staking contract.[file:7][file:11]  
- The certificate’s lien flag is cleared on‑chain, and it becomes **unencumbered**.[file:7][file:11]  

After lien release:

- Your certificate remains locked until its current unlock timestamp, but it is no longer tied to that lender.[file:7][file:11]  
- You may, subject to a new lender’s underwriting, pledge the same certificate as collateral for **another loan** with the same lender or a different participating lender without creating a new stake.[file:7][file:11]  
- Once the unlock date is reached and there are no active liens, you can unstake and withdraw the USDGB principal back into your wallet or choose to restake under a new term.[file:7][file:11]

## 9. Exiting Your Position

You can exit or reduce your USDGB exposure in several ways.

### 9.1 Selling on DEXs

Once USDGB is live on DEXs:

1. Connect your wallet to a supported DEX (e.g., Uniswap, Hyperliquid interface, Aerodrome, Jupiter route).[file:7][file:11]  
2. Select USDGB as the token you are selling.  
3. Choose your target asset (USDC, USDT, ETH, or other supported asset).  
4. Review the price and slippage, then confirm the swap in your wallet.[file:7]  

You can then send stablecoins to a centralized exchange that supports your jurisdiction and off‑ramp to fiat.[file:7][file:11]

### 9.2 Using Fiat Off‑Ramp Partners

Where supported, you may use listed off‑ramp partners to:

- Convert USDGB (directly or via USDC/USDT) to USD.  
- Withdraw to your linked bank account **within 1–3 business days**, depending on banking rails and jurisdiction.[file:7][file:8][file:11]  

KYC and bank‑account verification are required for these services.[file:7][file:8][file:11]

### 9.3 Unstaking After Unlock

When your staking certificate reaches its unlock date and no active liens are recorded:

1. Open the Goldbackbond dApp and connect your wallet.  
2. Go to **My Certificates** and find the certificate that has passed its unlock time.  
3. Click **Unstake / Withdraw**, confirm the transaction, and the staking contract will send the underlying USDGB back to your wallet.[file:7][file:11]  

You can then choose to hold, sell, or restake the tokens as desired.[file:7][file:11]
